This project is a Monte Carlo Portfolio Simulation System based on Tae Kim’s video, “9 Most Popular Investment Portfolio Strategies.” It uses historical market data to model and evaluate the long-term performance and risk characteristics of popular investment allocations. The program automatically downloads ten years of adjusted price data from Yahoo Finance, calculates each asset’s historical return, volatility, and correlation, and then runs thousands of simulated portfolio growth paths using a Monte Carlo process.

Each simulation compounds annually over a ten-year horizon, providing a statistically robust estimate of potential future outcomes if market conditions behave similarly to the past decade. The output includes key performance metrics such as mean and median portfolio value, volatility percentiles, Value at Risk (VaR), Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR), and the probability of loss relative to an initial $1 million investment.

The system produces both nominal results (raw, unadjusted portfolio values) and inflation-adjusted results, assuming a 2% annual inflation rate, allowing for a more realistic understanding of long-term purchasing power. Visualizations display the distribution of simulated outcomes for both scenarios, helping users compare nominal growth against real (inflation-adjusted) returns.

Currently, the project includes simulations for the Ivy League Endowment Strategy, Dave Ramsey 4-Fund Strategy, The Coffeehouse Strategy, and the Ultimate Buy and Hold Strategy, with five more portfolios yet to be added. The goal is to create a unified analytical framework to evaluate and compare multiple investment approaches under consistent, data-driven conditions.
